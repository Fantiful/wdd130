Overview

Purpose

The site would present my experience being a dungeon master and some of the things I have done or learned along the way. Its intended to be a showcase of my past projects and experiences.

Audience

It would be for new and old enjoyers of the Dungeons and Dragons. It demonstrates my creative abilities to transform concept into a fun and actionable experience for my players. Weaving together story telling elements and fun at the same time. It is also meant to inspire new and old Dungeon Masters or at the very least present them with ideas to contemplate.

Content

Home page

My Story: Who I am, how long I have been playing Dungeons & Dragons for, How I got into the game. Ultimately, It will share how my introduction and playstyle of the game was slightly unorthodox or different to those who typically play the game. However, this is meant to illustrate that the game is moldable and playable for anyone whether you are new to the game or looking for a new exciting experience. (Probably will be placed at the bottom of the page since most people don’t really care at first who you, but what you can offer them.) More so I want my work and purpose of the page to come first and then at the end introduce myself. Like a mysterious figure who reveals himself at the end.

What Seekest thou (Working title): A series of short questions with associated sneak peaks of information to come. For example, “Do you come seeking blood stained memories shrouded by the eternal winter?” (Insert symbol/button that would take the reader to the classics page where I will include the campaign, I ran titled “Blood in the Snow”. Then I would continue with other phrases “Or perhaps you seek an adventure where only you are needed?” (Again, I will insert a symbol/button link that takes the viewer to my Unique ideas page where they will be introduced to new ways of playing the game or resources that I have used.”

Classics

Three classics: I think I will most likely stick to sharing only three of my most successful campaigns I have written and run as a dungeon master. During which I will give a brief summary of the campaign, major themes, and how the campaign or idea was inspired.

Fellfall: This is one of my longest and most cherished campaigns I have ever run. (It’s technically still ongoing) due to how difficult it can be scheduled with everyone. However, the main premise of the story put the players in charge of running a crime ridden kingdom on the verge of collapse. In this world Kingdoms are a hierarchy system in a dying world surrounded slowly being engulfed in darkness.

Blood in the Snow: A vampire themed campaign. The players are set in a world thrust into eternal winter where overcast has become the new standard. Without the suns rays vampires run rampant, crops & wildlife begin to dwindle, and humanity has been thrusted into dire circumstances. It is a vampire apocalypse!

Hyperlinks: When referencing inspirations or resources I might place links within the text, on images, or go online to grab web icons as links. For example, icons of Pinterest or Mila note website since they are both resources I use and will most likely reference during my explanations and would like to make it easy for my readers to quickly and easy have access to those resources. (Making my site more valuable to viewers in the sense of being a quick reference sheet for dungeon master workflow. Or potential a toolbox that can be references.)

Keeping it unique

Two-four key concepts: I think this page will be the most important page to perfect and have really shine. This will be the most actionable page where readers will find resources I have either worked on and constructed myself, termed “Homebrew” in the dungeons & dragons community.

Journeying Solo: Sometimes you really get that itch to play Dungeons & Dragons. You’re excited, motivated, and even have a story prepared and ready to play/run. However, there is one common problem that plagues almost every enjoyer of the game. And that being the need for other players. Sometimes you don’t know anyone who plays the game or worse and more common in my experience, you have friends or know people who play the game but its nearly impossible to coordinate times that work for everyone. In this section I share resources and methods I have found that allow individuals to play and enjoy the game entirely alone.

Player Vs Player: A system where players get the opportunity to play and adventure against each other. An experimental gameplay system I am currently working on but have some prototype material for that style of D&D.
