Some would say an adventure isn’t a true adventure without the friends we made along the way. I would polity disagree and say that it was the experiences one had along the way since some people can’t be bothered to have a consistent schedule or cherish the amount of time and energy one puts into perfecting the perfect plot line with npcs, quest items, and (I’m just kidding)…. Maybe.

In actuality there have been many times when I and many other Dungeon Masters have worked hard on a campaign or have this super cool character, they really want to try only to have no one to play with. Or worse the dread no consistent schedule syndrome. Where no one is ever able to nail a consistent meeting time. Or perhaps you might be one of the lucky ones where you have a group that is consistent and meets regularly. However, you have been playing for some time now and are looking for something new and fresh to spice up your games.

As you venture deeper into the dungeon you will find adventures that only require one. Or perhaps you will soon discover that those who you once considered allies are now foes that grow in dark power and it’s up to you to prevent the fall of this brand-new world.

Though a lot of people have mixed opinions on this topic I would ask that you withhold judgement until you try it out for yourself. I have found that a great way to play Dungeons and Dragons alone is through using AI chat services as your dungeon Master such as ChatGPT. For this to work the very first thing you need to do is assign the AI its role. In our case you would write something like the following. “You are a experienced Dungeon Master that specializes in storytelling, mystery, suspense, fantasy, and creating a rich story driven environment.” This helps the AI understand what you expect of it and the role it is meant to play. Next you would write out a prompt that explains to the AI the world you want to play in, where you are starting in this world, your class, and level. From here the adventure begins and the AI will start based upon your instructions. For me personally I have a couple of additional rules to help keep the adventure exciting and avoid having the AI do everything, especially if you are a free user. For example, let’s say I enter the elder woods in search of some quest item. I will roll a D12 and depending on what I roll that can indicate the difficulty of an unexpected scenario or danger level. (I roll a 6, I tell AI that as I go deeper into the woods, an encounter occurs that is a 6/12 on difficult.) This allows me to be more engaged with the process and allows the adventure to feel more fluid and spontaneous as occurs with other real players. The AI can even generate monsters and help run battles as well. Just keep in mind that when using the free version of certain Chats you might have a limited amount of prompts per-day so sessions might have limited time like with real players and time constraints.

Have you ever looked at the player next to you and thought yourself “gosh if we weren’t forced to get along and not kill fellow party members, my character would total decimate your character.” Well never fear its time to put that theory to the test with my Player vs Player Dungeons and Dragons Prototype “Thousand-Year War”. The core idea of this game is that you, the player are fighting a thousand-year war between The Kingdom of Light and The Army of Darkness. Each side takes turns shaping the world. Your choices matter, and the world pushes back. Factions take turns going through the different phases, Upkeep Phase, Event Phase, Strategic Phase, Adventure Phase, Opposition Phase, Resolution Phase, and Reckoning Phase. Additionally, in this style of play there are two factions, “The Kingdom of Light” and “The Army of Darkness.” Depending on the number of players you can divide the teams up equally or more light to dark players depending on how you see fit. The reason this is important is due to ages. For Players of Light (Each player controls three heroes over time: Level 3 (Early Age) Level 5 (Middle Age) Level 8 (Late Age) Heroes may retire, rule, or die. Their deeds leave lasting legacies.) As for players of Darkness (Each player controls one level 10 character for the entire campaign. You grow stronger through titles, influence, and corruption. You shape the world indirectly as much as directly). The prototype can be found and accessed at the bottom of the page listed under (Versus D&D)
